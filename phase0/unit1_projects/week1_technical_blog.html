By Dylan Richards on Wednesday, April 23

Favorite Websites

Facebook | Google | Reddit

Breakdown

  • Facebook
  • This is one of my favorite websites because it allows me to keep in contact with people who I don't see every day.
  • My eyes are immediately drawn to the center -- the News Feed. After standing back, my eyes still drawn to the same place.
  • Simple, cluttered, basic, clean, interactive
  • The problem this site solves is lack of communication
  • Fun, serious, interaction, memorable, random
  • It's very easy to scroll down and view status updates
  • I feel bored and unproductive after being on the site for a while
  • I don't believe Facebook itself sells anything to social users, but I believe developers can pay Facebook to host games on its platform. That said, I have purchased countless game enhancements for Candy Crush.
  • Google
  • Google is one of my favorites sites because it puts the entire world at my fingertips. The answers to most of the questions I pose can be found in a Google search result
  • My eyes are drawn straight to the center of the page, where the search bar is located. It's practically the only thing on the page. When I step back, my eyes fixate in that direction. It is, indeed, the most important area of the site
  • Simple, minimal, basic, terse, effective
  • The website decreases the amount of time it takes for one to find the information he/she needs
  • Practical, informational, random, funny, serious
  • It's very easy to find what I'm looking for on Google. Everything's organized in a linear fashion
  • As easy as scrolling your mouse downward is
  • Interesting... I don't really know how I feel when I'm on Google for a long time. Perhaps it's beacuse I don't use Google for Google, per se, but for the results it provides
  • Like Facebook, Google doesn't sell anything to the typical user; however, it does sell ad space to developers
  • Reddit
  • Reddit is one of my favorite seites because it contains tons of exciting and interesting information about everything.
  • My eyes are drawn to the center of the page -- where all the content is.
  • Light, cluttered, square, complex
  • This website allows people from all over the world to discuss whatever they want in focused groups.
  • Funny, creepy, interesting, weird, scary
  • For a beginning user, it's pretty hard to find what you're looking for on Reddit.
  • This is pretty easy, though. Once you find the content you're looking for, all you've gotta do is scroll down.
  • Reddit sells what's known as Reddit Gold. It costs a few bucks and it allows users to show anonymous appreciation for users who post excellent content. Reddit Gold gives the recipient added features on the site.
