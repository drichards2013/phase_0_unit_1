What is the difference between margin, border, and padding?

The Box Model

The best way to explain border, margin, and padding is through the box model.

Each HTML element is contained within a rectangular box. In the box model, the element would be found in the area marked content.

The next most important thing to take note of is the border. Once we understand that elements are contained in rectangular boxes that have borders, we can begin to understand padding and margin.

Padding

Let's look at padding first. According to the box model, padding is the space between the border and the content. Therefore, the greater the padding, the more space between the content and the border.

Again, padding is the space between the content and the border.

Margin

If padding is the space between the border and the content, then margin is the space between the border and elements outside the box.
